Dark Matter and Dark Energy

Dark Matter and Dark Energy make up most of the universe's mass-energy content. Dark Matter acts as an invisible scaffold that structures the universe, while Dark Energy is responsible for its accelerated expansion.

Discovery

The existence of Dark Matter was first inferred by Fritz Zwicky in the 1930s through observations of galaxy clusters. The concept of Dark Energy came later, with evidence from supernova observations in the late 20th century suggesting an accelerating universe.

Characteristics

  • Dark Matter does not emit, absorb, or reflect light, making it invisible to current instruments.
  • Dark Energy's properties are still largely unknown, but it is pervasive throughout space.

Significance

Understanding Dark Matter and Dark Energy is essential for explaining the universe's structure and expansion history.

Properties of Dark Matter and Dark Energy
Aspect Dark Matter Dark Energy
Constitutes ~27% of the universe ~68% of the universe
Effect on Expansion Gravitational attraction slows it down Causes acceleration of the expansion
Detection Methods Gravitational lensing, galactic rotation curves Supernova cosmology, cosmic microwave background
